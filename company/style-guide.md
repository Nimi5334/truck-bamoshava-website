# Style Guide — Leverage Media

> Enforced by editor-in-chief at Gate 2. Violations are returned to the writer, not fixed silently,
> so writers learn (performance-coach tracks repeat violations).

## Voice rules
1. **Specific beats vivid.** "We cut research time from 6 hours to 40 minutes" — never "save tons of time."
2. **Numbers in every piece.** A claim without a number is an opinion; flag it as one or cut it.
3. **Short sentences carry the punch.** Average < 18 words. One idea per sentence.
4. **First person plural for company stories ("we ran…"), second person for playbooks ("you'll need…").**
5. **No hype vocabulary.** Banned: game-changer, revolutionary, unleash, supercharge, 10x (as adjective),
   "in today's fast-paced world", "the future of", unlock, seamless, delve, "it's important to note".
6. **Verbs do the selling.** "This cut our costs 40%" not "This is an amazing cost-saver."
7. **Honesty includes failure.** Every playbook names at least one thing that went wrong.
8. **No em-dash chains, no rhetorical question stacks, no "But here's the thing."** One rhetorical question per piece, max.

## Structure rules
- **Hook in the first 2 lines.** The reader decides in 8 seconds. Lead with the result or the tension.
- Longform: hook → promise → proof → playbook → pitfall → takeaway. Subheads every ~150 words.
- Shortform: hook line → meat → one concrete example → handle-free CTA.
- Every piece ends with ONE next action for the reader. Never two.

## Formatting
- Markdown. H2 for sections, H3 sparingly. Bold for the one key phrase per section, not decoration.
- Lists only when order or enumeration matters. Max 7 items.
- Links: descriptive anchor text, never "click here".

## Claims discipline (feeds Gate 3)
- Every factual claim needs an inline source marker: `[src: URL or "internal data <file>"]`.
  Fact-checker strips markers after verification; published copy is clean.
- Projections must say "we estimate" and show the assumption.

## Taboos
- Never invent metrics, testimonials, or anecdotes. Internal data comes from company/ files only.
- Never punch down. We mock hype, not people trying things.
- Never apologize for length or say "long post incoming."
